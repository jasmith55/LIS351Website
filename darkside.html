The Band:

Pink Floyd is a British progressive rock bandthat was most poular in the 70's, with a couple less popular albums in the late 60's and 80's and 90's. Their peak period of success was kickstarted with Dark Side of the Moon and continued with Wish You Were Here, Animals, and The Wall. Their most popular songs outside of this album are Wish You Were Here, Another Brick in the Wall Pt. 2, and Comfortably Numb.

Most Popular Songs:

Money
The most popular song on the ablum with over 300 million plays on Spotify. It uses coin-jingles and cash registers as parts of the song, and is about the use of money in society. It was one of two singles released for the album.
Time
The second most popular song on the album with over 200 million plays on Spotify. It is the fourth track on the album and begins abruptly with the alarming of clocks and alarms. It is also known to have one of the greatest guitar solos of all time.
Breathe
The third most popular song with over 160 million plays on Spotify. It's a more mellow song than the previous two and is about entering society.
And more...
Other great songs on this album are Brain Damage/Eclipse, The Great Gig In The Sky, and Us and Them

Why The Album Is Important

This album is one of the most iconic and best-selling albums of all time. Its combination of rock music and progressive elements makes it refreshing compared to the more straightforward rock albums of the time. It is also one of the few albums to have all songs lead into one another, making it feel more like an experience rather than a collection of individual songs. I'd always recognized this album cover growing up but didn't have the chance to listen to it until when I was around 13. It's a fantastic album and makes wise comments on society and our role as humans.
